# Module 1 — General: Foundations
*Read alongside AMTH General (FAA-H-8083-30B).*

## Learning Objectives
- Apply shop math & physics to maintenance problems.
- Identify aircraft **materials** and **hardware** and select/torque them correctly.
- Use **precision measuring tools** and read **drawings**.

## Lesson 1.1 — Shop Math & Physics
- Ratios, transposing formulas, area/volume, powers/roots, US↔metric.
- Physics you'll use: density/specific gravity (battery check), **gas laws**
  (P₁V₁/T₁=P₂V₂/T₂ — pressurization/pneumatics/oxygen), heat & thermal expansion (fits),
  force/work/power (W=F·d, P=W/t), Bernoulli & Pascal (lift, hydraulics).
- **Cylinder displacement** = (π/4)·bore²·stroke·#cylinders.

## Lesson 1.2 — Basic Aerodynamics (why the structure exists)
Four forces: lift, weight, thrust, drag. Airfoil terms (chord, camber, AoA, stall).
Control axes: pitch/roll/yaw. You maintain the machine that manages these — know them.

## Lesson 1.3 — Aircraft Drawings & Weight/Balance
- Read blueprints/schematics: title & revision blocks, tolerances, BOM; sketch repairs.
- **Weight & balance:** **CG = Σmoments ÷ Σweights**; empty-weight CG, useful load,
  weighing, equipment list. An out-of-CG aircraft is dangerous — mechanics update W&B
  after equipment changes.

## Lesson 1.4 — Materials & Processes
- **Aluminum alloys** (2024-T3, 7075), **Alclad** (corrosion-protected), **4130 steel**,
  titanium, magnesium. Alloy-temper designations tell you strength/heat-treat.
- **Composites:** fiberglass/carbon/honeycomb + resin; watch for delamination/disbond.
- **Heat treatment & hardness** — never substitute a "looks the same" part.

## Lesson 1.5 — Aircraft Hardware
- **Bolts/nuts/washers/screws** (AN/MS/NAS); **head markings** indicate strength.
- **Grip length** must match; **torque** to spec (never guess).
- **Positive locking:** safety wire, cotter pins, self-locking nuts, tab washers.
- **Rivets & special fasteners** intro (detailed in Module 3).
- **Fluid lines/fittings:** rigid tube vs. flex hose; **37° AN flare** vs. flareless MS;
  bend radius; fire-sleeve on flammable lines.

## Lesson 1.6 — Tools & Precision Measuring
- **Micrometer** (0.001"), **vernier/dial caliper**, dial indicator, feeler & thread
  gauges, **torque wrench** (and how to use an extension correctly), go/no-go gauges.
- Calibration and care.

## Key Formulas
| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| CG | Σmoments ÷ Σweights |
| Gas law | P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ |
| Displacement | (π/4)·bore²·stroke·#cyl |
| Work / Power | W=F·d ; P=W/t |

## Hands-On Labs
1. **Micrometer/caliper drill:** measure 5 objects to 0.001"; verify with a known gauge.
2. **Hardware ID:** given a mixed tray, sort AN bolts by grip/diameter and read head markings.
3. **Torque & safety wire:** torque a bolt to spec, then safety-wire it in the
   "pull-tight" direction (watch a free demo; practice on a fixture).
4. **W&B calc:** given weights & arms, compute CG and decide if within a given envelope.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. Convert: what is CG if total moment = 90,000 in-lb and total weight = 2,000 lb?
2. What does "-T3" indicate on 2024-T3 aluminum?
3. Why must bolt grip length match the joint?
4. Name three positive-locking methods.
5. What tool reads to 0.001"?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. 45 in (90,000/2,000). 2. A specific heat-treat temper condition. 3. So the bolt shank
(not threads) bears the shear load and the nut seats correctly. 4. Safety wire, cotter
pin, self-locking nut, tab washer (any three). 5. Micrometer.
</details>
