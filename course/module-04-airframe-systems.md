# Module 4 — Airframe: Systems
*Read alongside AMTH Airframe (FAA-H-8083-31B).*

## Learning Objectives
- Explain and service hydraulic/pneumatic, landing gear, and fuel systems.
- Describe environmental (pressurization/AC/oxygen), ice/rain, and fire systems.
- Understand airframe electrical, warning, avionics, and instrument systems + inspection.

## Lesson 4.1 — Hydraulic & Pneumatic Power
- **Fluids:** MIL-PRF-5606 (mineral, red), fire-resistant -83282/-87257, **Skydrol**
  (phosphate-ester — incompatible & corrosive; PPE!). **Never mix fluids.**
- Components: reservoir, pump, **accumulator**, selector/check/relief/priority valves,
  actuators, filters. Typical 1,500–3,000 psi.

## Lesson 4.2 — Landing Gear
- Fixed vs. retractable; tricycle vs. tailwheel.
- **Oleo shock struts:** service with nitrogen + hydraulic fluid; check extension.
- Retraction (hydraulic/electric), up/down locks, **squat/WOW switch**, emergency extension.
- **Brakes:** disc types, bleeding, anti-skid, wear pins. **Wheels/tires:** ply rating,
  **nitrogen** inflation, balance, inspection. **Nose steering & shimmy damper.**

## Lesson 4.3 — Fuel Systems
- Gravity vs. pump-fed; boost pumps, selectors, strainers/sumps, cross-feed.
- Tanks: integral (wet wing), bladder, rigid. **Water contamination** & sumping;
  leak classes (stain/seep/heavy seep/running). **Grades/color:** 100LL blue, Jet A straw.
- **Grounding/bonding** during fueling (static ignition prevention).

## Lesson 4.4 — Cabin Atmosphere & Environmental
- **Pressurization:** outflow valve, cabin-altitude control, differential limits, dump.
- **Heating** (combustion/bleed/exhaust-shroud — **CO risk**), **AC** (air-cycle "packs"
  & vapor-cycle), **oxygen** (gaseous/chemical; **no oil/grease near O₂ — fire hazard**).

## Lesson 4.5 — Ice/Rain, Fire, Electrical, Warning
- **Anti-ice** (prevents) vs. **de-ice** (removes): boots, TKS weeping wing, heated
  leading edges/pitot/props/windshield. Rain: wipers/repellent.
- **Fire protection:** loop/thermal detection, extinguisher bottles/squibs, discharge
  indicators, zones; test per manual.
- **Electrical distribution**, lighting, load analysis (wiring per AC 43.13-1B).
- **Warning systems:** gear position, stall/AoA, takeoff config, GPWS/TAWS, master caution.

## Lesson 4.6 — Avionics & Instruments (familiarization)
- **COM/NAV/transponder-ADS-B/ELT/GPS/autopilot/EFIS**; antennas, bonding for EMI, TSO/STC.
- **Pitot-static** (airspeed/altimeter/VSI) leak & certification (91.411/413); gyroscopic
  vs. electric; AHRS/ADC in glass cockpits.
- Deep avionics repair often needs a **repairman / repair-station** authorization.

## Lesson 4.7 — Airframe Inspection
- Zonal/checklist, annual/100-hr (Part 43 App. D), progressive; corrosion/structural
  surveys; life-limited tracking; compliant records.

## Hands-On Labs
1. **Strut service (sim/demo):** compute proper strut extension; service with N₂.
2. **Fuel sump check:** demonstrate sumping & identify water in a fuel sample.
3. **Hydraulic ID:** match fluid type to system; explain the danger of mixing Skydrol/mineral.
4. **Pitot-static leak check** (on a trainer/rig): interpret a leak-down result.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. Which hydraulic fluid is phosphate-ester and requires special PPE?
2. What gas inflates aircraft tires and struts, and why?
3. What is the CO hazard source in some cabin-heat systems?
4. Why must O₂ system fittings be free of oil/grease?
5. What switch tells the aircraft it's on the ground?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. Skydrol. 2. Nitrogen — inert (no moisture/oxidation, stable pressure vs. temperature).
3. Exhaust heat-exchanger (muff/shroud) leaks. 4. Oil/grease + high-pressure O₂ can
auto-ignite/explode. 5. The squat / weight-on-wheels (WOW) switch.
</details>
