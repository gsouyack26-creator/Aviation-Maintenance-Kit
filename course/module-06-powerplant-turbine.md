# Module 6 — Powerplant: Turbine Engines
*Read alongside AMTH Powerplant (FAA-H-8083-32B).*

## Learning Objectives
- Explain the Brayton cycle and gas-turbine sections & types.
- Describe turbine fuel, ignition, oil, and instrumentation.
- Understand turbine inspection/troubleshooting basics.

## Lesson 6.1 — Principle & Sections
- **Brayton cycle:** intake → compress → burn (constant pressure) → expand/exhaust.
- **Sections:** inlet → **compressor** (axial or centrifugal) → diffuser → **combustor**
  → **turbine** → exhaust/nozzle; plus accessory gearbox.
- **Spools:** N1 (fan/LP), N2 (HP). **Hot section** (combustor + turbine) vs. cold section.

## Lesson 6.2 — Types
- **Turbojet** (pure thrust), **turbofan** (bypass; airliners), **turboprop** (drives a
  prop via reduction gear), **turboshaft** (helicopters/APUs). Thrust vs. shaft power.

## Lesson 6.3 — Systems
- **Fuel:** fuel control unit (FCU) or **FADEC**, nozzles/manifold, fuel heat.
- **Ignition:** high-energy capacitor-discharge igniters (start & continuous modes only).
- **Oil:** dry-sump synthetic; scavenge; chip detectors.
- **Instruments:** N1/N2, **ITT/EGT** (limits!), torque (turboprop), fuel flow.
- Materials: nickel superalloys, single-crystal blades, thermal-barrier coatings.

## Lesson 6.4 — Inspection & Troubleshooting
- **Borescope** the hot section (blades/vanes for cracks, burn, erosion).
- **FOD** at the inlet; **compressor wash** for performance recovery.
- Start faults: **hot start** (over-temp), **hung start** (won't accelerate), **wet start**
  (no light-off). EGT/vibration **trend monitoring**.

## Key Concepts
| Term | Meaning |
|---|---|
| N1 / N2 | Low- / high-pressure spool speed (% RPM) |
| ITT / EGT | Turbine gas temperature — a critical limit |
| EPR | Engine pressure ratio (thrust indicator) |
| Bypass ratio | Fan air ÷ core air (efficiency of a turbofan) |

## Hands-On Labs
1. **Airflow trace:** on a turbofan cutaway/animation, follow air through each section.
2. **Borescope (video/trainer):** identify a cracked vs. healthy turbine blade.
3. **Start-fault ID:** given EGT/RPM traces, classify hot vs. hung vs. wet start.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. Name the gas-turbine sections in order.
2. Which engine type powers most modern airliners and why?
3. What does an over-temperature during start indicate?
4. What does a borescope let you inspect without teardown?
5. What is N1 vs. N2?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. Inlet, compressor, (diffuser), combustor, turbine, exhaust. 2. Turbofan — high bypass
gives better fuel efficiency and lower noise. 3. A hot start. 4. The internal hot
section (blades/vanes/combustor) through access ports. 5. Low-pressure (fan) spool speed
vs. high-pressure spool speed.
</details>
