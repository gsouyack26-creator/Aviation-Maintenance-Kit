# Module 3 — Airframe: Structures & Sheet Metal
*Read alongside AMTH Airframe (FAA-H-8083-31B) + AC 43.13-1B.*

## Learning Objectives
- Identify airframe structure types and how loads are carried.
- Lay out and perform a **sheet-metal riveted repair** to standard.
- Understand composite, welded, fabric repair and **control rigging**.

## Lesson 3.1 — Structures
- **Fuselage:** truss (welded tube), monocoque (skin carries load), **semi-monocoque**
  (skin + formers/longerons/stringers — modern standard).
- **Wing:** spars, ribs, stringers, stressed skin; wet-wing tanks.
- **Loads:** tension, compression, shear, bending, torsion. **Station numbering** locates parts.

## Lesson 3.2 — Sheet-Metal Layout & Forming
- **Bend Allowance (BA)** = (0.01743·R + 0.0078·T)·N° (R=inside radius, T=thickness, N=angle).
- **Setback** (90° bend) = R + T. Sight lines, brake bending, joggles, minimum bend radius.

## Lesson 3.3 — Rivets & Riveted Repairs
- Solid rivets: **AN470 universal**, **AN426 100° flush**. Code letters give alloy/head:
  **AD = 2117**, **DD = 2024 ("ice-box," heat-treat/refrigerate)**.
- Sizing rules of thumb:
  - **Diameter ≈ 3 × sheet thickness**
  - **Length = grip + 1.5 × diameter** (for the shop head)
  - **Edge distance 2–2.5 D**, **pitch 4–6 D**, **transverse pitch ≥ 2.5 D**
  - **Shop head:** 1.5 D wide × 0.5 D high (inspect with a rivet gauge)
- **Blind rivets / Hi-Loks / Cherrymax** where you can't buck.
- **Repair rule:** restore/exceed original strength & cross-section, never reduce edge
  distance; use SRM or AC 43.13-1B. **Stop-drill** crack ends.

## Lesson 3.4 — Composites, Welding, Fabric
- **Composites:** laminate/honeycomb, ply orientation, tap/UT for disbond; scarf/step-lap
  patch, wet layup vs. prepreg, vacuum-bag & heat cure.
- **Welding:** TIG (GTAW) dominant; 4130 tube clusters; inspect penetration/porosity.
- **Fabric:** covering methods, punch-test strength, patch repair.
- **Transparent plastics:** stop-drill cracks; never clean with petroleum solvents.

## Lesson 3.5 — Assembly & Rigging
- **Rigging** aligns/adjusts flight controls to spec.
- Cable systems: **tensiometer** (temp-corrected), turnbuckles safetied, pulleys/fairleads.
- **Control surface travel** set with protractors/fixtures; **balance** (static/dynamic)
  after repair/paint to prevent **flutter**.

## Key Formulas
| Quantity | Formula / rule |
|---|---|
| Bend allowance | (0.01743R+0.0078T)·N° |
| Setback (90°) | R + T |
| Rivet diameter | ≈ 3 × thickness |
| Rivet length | grip + 1.5D |
| Edge distance / pitch | 2–2.5D / 4–6D |
| Shop head | 1.5D wide × 0.5D high |

## Hands-On Labs
1. **Layout lab:** compute BA & setback for a 90° bend in 0.040" 2024-T3, R=0.156"; mark bend/sight lines.
2. **Riveting lab:** drill, deburr, cleco, and buck a 4-rivet lap joint; inspect shop
   heads to 1.5D×0.5D and correct edge distance.
3. **Rigging demo:** set/measure cable tension with a tensiometer; check a control-surface
   travel range with a protractor.
4. **Composite tap test:** find the disbond in a sample honeycomb panel.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. For 0.050" skin, what solid-rivet diameter is typical?
2. What is minimum edge distance in terms of D?
3. What does a "DD" rivet require before driving?
4. Why balance a control surface after repainting?
5. Compute setback for a 90° bend, R=0.125", T=0.040".

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. ≈3×0.050 = 0.150" → 5/32" (–5) rivet. 2. 2–2.5 D. 3. It's 2024 ("ice-box"):
heat-treat then keep refrigerated and drive before it ages/hardens. 4. To prevent
flutter (added paint weight shifts the balance). 5. R+T = 0.125+0.040 = 0.165".
</details>
