# Module 2 — General: Electricity & Inspection
*Read alongside AMTH General (FAA-H-8083-30B), electrical & inspection chapters.*

## Learning Objectives
- Analyze DC/AC circuits and aircraft wiring/battery systems.
- Perform visual and **non-destructive testing (NDT)** inspections.
- Identify and treat **corrosion**; handle fire & HAZMAT safely.

## Lesson 2.1 — Electrical Theory
- **Ohm's Law: E=I·R. Power: P=I·E = I²·R.** Series vs. parallel circuits.
- AC theory basics, capacitance/inductance, transformers, rectifiers.

## Lesson 2.2 — Aircraft Electrical Hardware
- **Batteries:** lead-acid vs. **Ni-Cad** (thermal-runaway awareness), capacity, charging,
  electrolyte/specific-gravity checks.
- **Generation/regulation:** generators/alternators, voltage regulators, bus systems,
  external power, inverters.
- **Wiring:** AWG sizing (current + length + allowable drop), bundling, chafe protection,
  connectors, **circuit protection** (fuses/breakers). Read schematics.

## Lesson 2.3 — Inspection Concepts
- **Visual** first (borescope, mirror, magnifier, good light).
- **Required inspections:** annual, 100-hour, progressive; use checklists (Part 43 App. D).

## Lesson 2.4 — Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)
| Method | Detects | Best on |
|---|---|---|
| **Dye/Fluorescent Penetrant (PT)** | Surface cracks | Non-porous metals & composites |
| **Magnetic Particle (MT)** | Surface/near-surface flaws | Ferrous (magnetic) parts |
| **Eddy Current (ET)** | Surface/subsurface cracks, conductivity | Conductive metals |
| **Ultrasonic (UT)** | Internal flaws, thickness | Most solids |
| **Radiographic (RT)** | Internal structure | Specialized/licensed |

## Lesson 2.5 — Corrosion Control
- **Types:** surface, pitting, intergranular, exfoliation, filiform, stress, fretting,
  **galvanic** (dissimilar metals), microbial (fuel tanks).
- **Prevention/treatment:** clean, protective finishes/CPC, drainage, sealants; neutralize
  & re-protect before repainting. Dissimilar-metal isolation.

## Lesson 2.6 — Fire Safety & HAZMAT
- **Fire classes A/B/C/D** and matching extinguishers.
- SDS sheets, PPE, safe handling of fuels/solvents/battery acid/composite dust; disposal.

## Key Formulas
| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Ohm's Law | E = I·R |
| Power | P = I·E = I²·R |

## Hands-On Labs
1. **Circuit lab:** build a series & a parallel circuit; measure V/I/R and confirm Ohm's law.
2. **Penetrant inspection:** run a dye-penetrant check on a cracked scrap part; interpret.
3. **Corrosion ID:** photograph/label corrosion types on scrap aluminum; treat one sample.
4. **Battery service:** measure specific gravity / voltage; interpret state of charge.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. A 28 V bus drives a 14 Ω load. What current flows? What power is dissipated?
2. Which NDT method suits a crack in a steel (ferrous) fitting?
3. What corrosion occurs between dissimilar metals in contact?
4. Which fire extinguisher class is for energized electrical fires?
5. What's the first, most-used inspection method?

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. I=E/R=28/14=2 A; P=I·E=2×28=56 W. 2. Magnetic particle (MT). 3. Galvanic corrosion.
4. Class C. 5. Visual inspection.
</details>
