# Module 0 — Orientation & Setup

## Learning Objectives
- Understand what an **A&P mechanic** is and the three knowledge domains.
- Download your free "textbooks."
- Adopt the aviation-maintenance safety & documentation mindset.

## Lesson 0.1 — The A&P world
An aircraft mechanic in the U.S. holds an **FAA Mechanic Certificate** with **Airframe**
and/or **Powerplant** ratings ("A&P"). Knowledge splits into:
- **General** — shared foundation (math, physics, materials, electricity, tools, inspection).
- **Airframe** — the structure and non-engine systems.
- **Powerplant** — engines and propellers.

Aviation maintenance differs from industrial maintenance in one huge way: **everything is
governed by regulation and traceable to approved data, and every action is documented.**
A missed step can be fatal, so the culture is procedure- and record-driven.

## Lesson 0.2 — Get your free books (do this now)
From the FAA Aviation Handbooks page, download:
1. **FAA-H-8083-30B** — AMTH General
2. **FAA-H-8083-31B** — AMTH Airframe
3. **FAA-H-8083-32B** — AMTH Powerplant

Page: https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation
Also grab **AC 43.13-1B** (Acceptable Methods, Techniques & Practices) — your shop bible:
https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/advisory_circulars

Bookmark: **eCFR Title 14** (https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14) and **FAA DRS**
(https://drs.faa.gov/) for regulations and Airworthiness Directives.

## Lesson 0.3 — Safety & documentation mindset
- **FOD (Foreign Object Damage/Debris):** account for every tool and part — a loose bolt
  in a control system or engine can destroy an aircraft. Practice **tool control**.
- **The Dirty Dozen** human-factors errors cause most maintenance mistakes: communication,
  complacency, knowledge, distraction, teamwork, fatigue, resources, pressure,
  assertiveness, stress, awareness, norms.
- **If it isn't documented, it didn't happen** — you will learn to write compliant records.
- **Use approved data** — never "wing it"; follow the manual, SRM, or AC.

## Self-Check Quiz
1. What do the "A" and "P" in A&P stand for?
2. Name the three A&P knowledge domains.
3. Which single AC is the go-to for accepted repair methods?
4. What does "FOD" mean and why does it matter?
5. Name three of the "Dirty Dozen."

<details><summary>Answers</summary>
1. Airframe and Powerplant. 2. General, Airframe, Powerplant. 3. AC 43.13-1B.
4. Foreign Object Damage/Debris — loose items can jam controls or wreck engines.
5. Any of: communication, complacency, knowledge, distraction, teamwork, fatigue,
resources, pressure, assertiveness, stress, awareness, norms.
</details>
