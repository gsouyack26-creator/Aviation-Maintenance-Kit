# 02 — General Subjects (FAA-H-8083-30B knowledge base)

> The foundation every mechanic shares before specializing in Airframe or Powerplant.
> Source of record: **AMTH – General**, free from the FAA. [[E1]](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation)

---

## G-1. Mathematics
- **Arithmetic & algebra:** ratios, percentages, transposing formulas.
- **Geometry/area/volume:** wing area, tank capacity, cylinder displacement.
- **Powers/roots & the metric ↔ US conversion** are used constantly.
- **Displacement of one cylinder** = π/4 × bore² × stroke. Total = × #cylinders.

## G-2. Physics
- **Matter & states**, **density / specific gravity** (battery electrolyte check).
- **Force, work (W=F·d), power (P=W/t), energy** (potential/kinetic).
- **Gas laws** — Boyle's (P₁V₁=P₂V₂), Charles', combined & general gas law — the basis of
  pressurization, pneumatics, and oxygen systems.
- **Heat:** conduction/convection/radiation; thermal expansion (why fits & clearances matter).
- **Bernoulli / Pascal / mechanical advantage** (hydraulics, jacks, control systems).

## G-3. Basic Aerodynamics
- Four forces: **lift, weight, thrust, drag.**
- Airfoil terms: chord, camber, angle of attack, center of pressure, stall.
- Axes & controls: **pitch (elevator), roll (aileron), yaw (rudder)**; stability concepts.

## G-4. Aircraft Drawings
- Read **blueprints, schematics, and diagrams**; title blocks, revision blocks, tolerances,
  bill of materials. **Sketching** repairs to scale for approval.

## G-5. Weight & Balance
- **CG (center of gravity)** must stay within limits.
- **Moment = weight × arm; CG = total moment ÷ total weight.**
- Empty-weight CG, useful load, weighing procedures, equipment list, loading limits.
- Ref: Weight & Balance Handbook (FAA-H-8083-1B). [[E1]](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation)

## G-6. Aircraft Materials, Hardware & Processes
- **Metals:** aluminum alloys (2024, 7075 + clad "Alclad"), steel alloys (4130 chromoly),
  titanium, magnesium. Alloy/temper designations (e.g., **2024-T3**).
- **Heat treatment**, hardness testing, and why you never substitute hardware casually.
- **Composites:** fiberglass, carbon fiber, honeycomb core, resins — inspection for
  delamination/disbond by tap test & instruments.
- **Aircraft hardware:** AN/MS/NAS **bolts, nuts, washers, screws, rivets, turnlock &
  cowl fasteners**; **fastener grip length & torque**; **safety wire & cotter pins**
  (positive locking). Bolt head markings identify strength.

## G-7. Fluid Lines & Fittings
- **Rigid tubing** (aluminum/steel) vs. **flexible hose** (rubber/Teflon).
- **Flare (37° AN vs 45°) and flareless (MS)** fittings; bend radius; support clamps.
- Fluid-line **color-coded** identification; fire-sleeve on flammable-fluid lines.

## G-8. Cleaning & Corrosion Control
- **Corrosion types:** surface, pitting, intergranular, exfoliation, filiform, stress,
  fretting, galvanic (**dissimilar metals**), microbial (in fuel tanks).
- Prevention: **cleaning, protective finishes, drainage, sealants, corrosion-preventive
  compounds (CPC)**; treat & neutralize before repainting.

## G-9. Ground Operation & Servicing
- Safe **starting, run-up, taxi signals, tie-down, jacking, mooring**, towing.
- **Servicing:** fueling (grounding/bonding to prevent static ignition), oil, oxygen,
  hydraulic fluid, tires/struts (nitrogen), de-ice fluid.
- **Fire on the ramp**, fuel spill, and hand-signal safety.

## G-10. Aircraft Electricity & Electronics
- **Ohm's Law: E = I × R; Power: P = I × E.** Series vs. parallel circuits.
- **DC & AC theory**, capacitance, inductance, transformers, rectifiers.
- **Batteries:** lead-acid vs. **Ni-Cad** (thermal runaway awareness), capacity, charging.
- **Generators/alternators, voltage regulation, bus systems, circuit protection**
  (fuses, breakers), **wiring** (AWG sizing, bundling, chafe protection), connectors,
  and **schematic reading**.

## G-11. Inspection Concepts & Techniques (incl. NDT)
- **Visual** (most common) with borescope, mirror, magnifier.
- **Non-Destructive Testing (NDT):**
  - **Dye/Fluorescent Penetrant (PT)** — surface cracks in non-porous parts.
  - **Magnetic Particle (MT)** — surface & near-surface flaws in *ferrous* parts.
  - **Eddy Current (ET)** — surface/subsurface cracks, conductivity, coating thickness.
  - **Ultrasonic (UT)** — internal flaws & thickness.
  - **Radiographic (RT / X-ray)** — internal structure (specialized, licensed).
- Understand **required inspections** (annual, 100-hour, progressive) and checklists
  (Part 43 Appendix D). [[E2]](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-43)

## G-12. Hand Tools & Measuring
- Layout, cutting, drilling, riveting tools; torque wrenches.
- **Precision measuring:** steel rule, **micrometer** (0.001"), **vernier/dial caliper**,
  dial indicator, thread & feeler gauges, **go/no-go**. Calibration matters.

## G-13. Maintenance Publications, Forms & Records
- Manufacturer **Maintenance Manuals, IPC (parts catalog), SRM (structural repair
  manual), wiring diagrams, ADs, SBs, TCDS**.
- FAA **Form 337** (major repair/alteration), logbook entries per §43.9. [[E2]](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-43)

## G-14. Mechanic Privileges & Limitations
- What an A&P may sign off, what needs an **IA**, when manufacturer data is required,
  and **"return to service"** responsibility (Part 65 / Part 43 §43.7). [[E3]](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-D/part-65)

## G-15. Human Factors & Maintenance Safety
- The **"Dirty Dozen"** error precursors: lack of communication, complacency, lack of
  knowledge, distraction, lack of teamwork, fatigue, lack of resources, pressure, lack of
  assertiveness, stress, lack of awareness, norms.
- **SMS** (Safety Management Systems), fatigue/shift risk, tool control (FOD), and
  **maintenance resource management (MRM).**

## G-16. Fire Safety & Hazardous Materials
- **Fire classes A/B/C/D** and correct extinguishing agents.
- **HAZMAT / hazcom:** SDS sheets, PPE, solvent & fuel handling, battery acid,
  composite dust, and proper disposal.

| Quantity | Formula |
|---|---|
| Ohm's Law | E = I·R |
| Electrical power | P = I·E = I²·R |
| CG | Σ moments ÷ Σ weights |

| Gas (combined) | P₁V₁/T₁ = P₂V₂/T₂ |
| Work / Power | W=F·d ; P=W/t |
