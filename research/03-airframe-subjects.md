# 03 — Airframe Subjects (FAA-H-8083-31B knowledge base)

> The structure and non-engine systems of the aircraft. Source of record: **AMTH –
> Airframe**, free from the FAA. [[E1]](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/handbooks_manuals/aviation)

---

## A-1. Aircraft Structures
- **Major components:** fuselage, wings, empennage (horizontal/vertical stabilizer),
  flight controls, landing gear, powerplant/nacelle.
- **Fuselage types:** **truss** (welded steel tube), **monocoque** (skin carries load),
  **semi-monocoque** (skin + frames/formers/longerons/stringers — the modern standard).
- **Wing structure:** spars, ribs, stringers, skin; **stressed-skin** design; wet-wing
  fuel tanks. Loads: tension, compression, shear, bending, torsion.
- **Station numbering** (fuselage/wing/butt lines) locates components & damage.

## A-2. Assembly & Rigging
- **Rigging** = aligning/adjusting flight controls to manufacturer specs.
- **Flight control systems:** cable (with turnbuckles, pulleys, fairleads), push-pull rod,
  torque tube, and fly-by-wire.
- **Cable tension** set with a **tensiometer**, corrected for temperature; turnbuckles
  safetied (safety wire or clips). **Control surface travel** checked with protractors/rigging fixtures.
- **Control surface balancing** (static/dynamic) prevents flutter — critical after painting/repair.
- **Primary controls:** aileron, elevator, rudder. **Secondary:** flaps, slats, trim tabs,
  spoilers.

## A-3. Fabric Covering & Wood/Structure Repair
- **Fabric** (cotton/Ceconite/polyester): covering methods, dope vs. modern systems,
  rejuvenation, patch repair, punch-test for strength.
- **Wood structures** (vintage): spar splices, glue joints, allowable defects, moisture.

## A-4. Aircraft Metal Structural Repair (sheet metal — big topic)
- **Layout & forming:** bend allowance, setback, sight lines, brake bending, joggling.
  - **Bend Allowance (BA)** = the material consumed in a bend =
    (0.01743 × R + 0.0078 × T) × N degrees (R=inside radius, T=thickness, N=bend angle).
  - **Setback** for a 90° bend = R + T.
- **Rivets:** solid (AN470 universal, AN426 100° flush), **rivet code** identifies
  alloy/head (e.g., **AD = 2117**, **DD = 2024 "ice-box"**). Diameter ≈ 3× skin thickness;
  length = grip + 1.5×D for the shop head.
- **Rivet layout rules of thumb:** **edge distance 2–2.5D**, **pitch 4–6D**, transverse
  (gauge) pitch **≥ 2.5D**. Shop head standard **1.5D wide × 0.5D high**.
- **Blind rivets, Hi-Loks, Cherrymax** where a bucking bar can't reach.
- **Repair philosophy:** restore original strength, match/exceed cross-sectional area,
  never reduce edge distance; follow the **Structural Repair Manual (SRM)** or
  **AC 43.13-1B** when no data exists. [[E2]](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/advisory_circulars)
- Damage classification: negligible, repairable, replace. **Stop-drilling** cracks.

## A-5. Welding
- **Oxy-acetylene, TIG (GTAW), MIG** — TIG dominant for aircraft steel/aluminum.
- Weld types, joints, inspection for penetration/porosity; **4130 chromoly** tube clusters.

## A-6. Composite & Plastic Repair
- **Laminates & sandwich (honeycomb) construction**, ply orientation, resin systems.
- Damage detection: **tap test, ultrasonic, thermography**; disbond/delamination.
- Repair: scarf/step-lap patches, wet layup vs. prepreg, vacuum bag & heat cure.
- **Transparent plastics** (windshields/windows): acrylic; crazing, stop-drilling cracks,
  cleaning (never with petroleum solvents).

## A-7. Painting & Finishing
- Surface prep (alodine/conversion coating, primer), topcoats, corrosion protection,
  **control-surface rebalance** after repaint, registration marking rules.

## A-8. Hydraulic & Pneumatic Power Systems
- **Fluids:** MIL-PRF-5606 (mineral, red), -83282/-87257 (fire-resistant), Skydrol
  (phosphate-ester — **incompatible**, corrosive to skin/paint — PPE!).
- Components: reservoir, pump, **accumulator, selector/check/relief/priority valves,
  actuators, filters**. Pressures often 1,500–3,000 psi (some 5,000 psi).
- **Pneumatics** for high-pressure systems, brakes, door seals, some deice.

## A-9. Landing Gear Systems
- **Fixed vs. retractable**; tricycle vs. tailwheel.
- **Oleo (air/oil) shock struts** — servicing (nitrogen + hydraulic fluid), extension checks.
- **Retraction systems** (hydraulic/electric), up/down locks, **squat/WOW switch**,
  emergency extension, position & warning.
- **Brakes:** single/multiple-disc, self-adjusting, bleeding, anti-skid; **wear-pin** checks.
- **Wheels & tires:** bias vs. radial, ply rating, **nitrogen** inflation, balance,
  retread limits, inspection for cuts/flat spots.
- **Nose-wheel steering & shimmy dampers.**

## A-10. Fuel Systems
- Gravity vs. pump-fed; **boost pumps, selector valves, strainers/sumps, cross-feed**.
- **Fuel tanks:** integral (wet wing), bladder, rigid removable. Sealants, leak
  classification (stain/seep/heavy seep/running). **Water contamination** & sumping.
- **Fuel grades/color:** 100LL (blue), Jet A (clear/straw). Never mis-fuel. Grounding/bonding.

## A-11. Cabin Atmosphere Control
- **Pressurization** (isobaric/differential, outflow valve, cabin-altitude control),
  structural pressure limits, dump valves.
- **Heating** (combustion heaters, bleed air, exhaust shrouds — CO risk), **air
  conditioning** (vapor-cycle & air-cycle "packs"), **oxygen systems** (gaseous vs.
  chemical, no oil/grease near O₂ — fire hazard!).

## A-12. Ice & Rain Control
- **Anti-ice** (prevents) vs. **de-ice** (removes): pneumatic **boots**, **TKS weeping
  wing**, electric/bleed-air heated leading edges, heated pitot/props/windshield.
- **Rain:** wipers, rain repellent, pneumatic rain removal.

## A-13. Fire Protection Systems
- **Detection:** thermal switch, thermocouple, continuous-loop (Kidde/Fenwal), smoke/CO detectors.
- **Extinguishing:** Halon (legacy)/Halon-replacement bottles, squibs, discharge indicators,
  zones (engine/APU/cargo/lav). Test per manual.

## A-14. Aircraft Electrical Systems (airframe side)
- Power generation/distribution, bus tie, external power, inverters, **circuit protection
  & load analysis**, lighting (nav/anti-collision/landing), wiring per **AC 43.13-1B ch.11**. [[E2]](https://www.faa.gov/regulations_policies/advisory_circulars)

## A-15. Position & Warning Systems
- **Landing-gear position, stall warning, AoA, takeoff config, GPWS/TAWS, overspeed**,
  anti-skid, master caution/warning logic.

## A-16. Communication & Navigation (avionics familiarization)
- **COM (VHF/HF), NAV (VOR/ILS/GPS/GNSS), transponder/ADS-B, ELT, DME, ADF, RADAR,
  autopilot, EFIS/glass cockpit**, antennas, static/bonding for interference, TSO/STC
  install rules. Deep avionics repair often requires a **repairman/repair-station** cert.

## A-17. Aircraft Instrument Systems
- **Pitot-static** (airspeed/altimeter/VSI) — leak checks, static-system certification
  (91.411/91.413). **Gyroscopic** (attitude/heading/turn), vacuum vs. electric, **AHRS/ADC**
  in glass cockpits. Instrument markings/ranges.

## A-18. Airframe Inspection
- Zonal & checklist inspections, **annual/100-hr** (Part 43 App. D), progressive programs,
  corrosion & structural surveys, life-limited component tracking, and compliant records. [[E3]](https://www.ecfr.gov/current/title-14/chapter-I/subchapter-C/part-43)
